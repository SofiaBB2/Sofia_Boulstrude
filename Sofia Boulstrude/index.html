About Boulstrude

Singer-Songwriter | Multi-instrumentalist | Creative Researcher

Sofia Boulstrude is a 17-year-old Brazilian artist whose work resides at the intersection of scientific inquiry and raw emotional storytelling. Currently a student of Informatics at the Federal Institute of Paraná (IFPR), she weaves the logic of code and the mysteries of cosmology into a soundscape that is both cerebral and deeply intimate.

The Sound & Vision

Starting her journey as an acoustic singer-songwriter, Sofia has evolved from the delicate strums of the ukulele and guitar to a more complex, "chamber-folk" and experimental sound. Her recent work integrates the haunting resonance of the cello with the rhythmic precision of electronic textures and piano arpejos. Her lyrical themes are often metaphors for identity and the passage of time. She draws inspiration from astronomy and physics to describe human reactions, waste, and the vastness of the cosmos, documenting the transition from childhood to her "grown self," particularly through the lens of grief and the legacies left by those who shaped her.

Projects & Milestones

Sofia’s artistic path is complemented by her academic and community contributions:

Discography: Author of several unpublished EPs, such as Peaceful and The Eclipse, she hopes to soon produce them properly.

Full-lengh album: Currently developing All The Ways I Know of Haunting (ATWIKoH), a visceral exploration of memory and loss.

Beyond Music: A bronze medalist in the Brazilian Mathematics Olympiad (OBMEP) and lead developer of ResiduoLab ChemSafe, a project managing chemical waste through technology.

Whether performing in youth choirs, writing opinion articles for local newspapers, or experimenting with the cello in her bedroom, Sofia Boulstrude remains a "testimony of layers" — always digging deeper to find the resonance between the heart and the stars.
